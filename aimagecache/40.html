    Blendtec Designer Series Blender Review

    To purchase your Blendtec Designer Series Blender right now, click here.

    My first high-end blender was a Blendtec. While I was blown away by the power and ease of use of my trusty old Blendtec Total Blender (Classic Series), the new Designer Series really classes up my kitchen.

    With a combination of golf ball-pulverizing power and sleek, touch interface, the Blendtec Designer Series blender is a joy to use and easily whips up silky smooth green smoothies.

    What Does The Blendtec Designer Series Blender Offer?

    The Blendtec Designer Series blender has a powerful three peak horsepower, 1560 watt motor. The motor is powerful enough to blend up the smoothest green smoothies without leaving any bits of fruit or greens intact. It has no problem liquifying pineapple core, raw carrot, and tough greens like kale.

    If you’ve ever watched Blendtec’s “Will It Blend” video series on YouTube, you know that if the occasion ever required it, you could probably turn a garden rake or your iPhone into fine powder (while voiding your warranty, of course).

    Blendtec blenders have a single wingtip blade that makes short work of anything that you put in your blender pitcher. The blade relies on speed to pulverize ingredients, which it does exceptionally well. Because of this, it is not sharp so you won’t cut yourself when cleaning out the blender.

    The blender comes with a large, 90-ounce BPA-free square WildSide pitcher (there are markings on the pitcher up to 32 ounces, but the total volume is 90 ounces). A smaller FourSide pitcher is also available with a total volume of almost 64 ounces. I highly recommend going with the larger WildSide pitcher since it blends much better and is easier to use. (The Designer Series blender comes with the WildSide pitcher).

    Blendtec also makes a smaller pitcher called the Twister Jar. The Twister Jar is a fantastic accessory for your Blendtec blender, especially if you regularly make nut butters. With the Twister Jar, you can whip up a nut butter in about a minute, and vegan, frozen fruit-based ice creams come out perfectly creamy every time.

    The Blendtec Designer Series blender features a sleek, ultra-modern touch interface. There are no switches to flip, no buttons to push, and no dials to turn. Simply tap the desired pre-set blending icon (“Smoothies”, for instance) and let the blender do its thing. No need to hold down the lid or do anything else!

    You can also fine tune your blends by swiping your finger back and forth along the interface to speed up or slow down a blend.

    The Blendtec Designer Series blender with WildSide pitcher stands about 15 inches tall. The biggest plus with Blendtec blenders is that they are fairly compact and will fit under your kitchen cabinets. They also take up much less space on your counter than other high-end blenders.

    The Blendtec Designer Series blender comes with a 7-year warranty. My old Blendtec Total Blender (Classic Series) has stood up to an average of 8 uses a day for almost 4 years and it’s still going strong. I would expect this blender to outlast most of my other kitchen appliances.

    The Blendtec Designer Series blender starts at $454.95 in black, cream and red finish (champagne metallic finish is $499.95). The blender is well worth the investment when you consider that it should last at least a decade or more with normal use.

    How The Blendtec Designer Series Blender Compares To The Vitamix 5200/6300 Blender

    I wrote an in depth article comparing these two blenders from a technical perspective a couple years ago. You can read it here.

    However, I will note a few of the obvious differences between the two blenders below.

    First of all, there is absolutely no quality difference between a green smoothie made in a Blendtec blender or a Vitamix blender. There is no noticeable quality or texture difference between the two blenders.

    One of the things I LOVE about Blendtec blenders is the pre-set buttons. It’s nice to hit the “Smoothie” button and then clean up while the blender blends automatically. With the Vitamix 5200 blender, you have to “babysit” the blender and use the tamper or nothing will blend. Vitamix is much more hands-on.

    The newer and more expensive Vitamix 6300 blender has only three pre-programed blend buttons, one of which is for smoothies.

    The Blendtec Designer Series blender is louder than the Vitamix. It’s supposed to be quieter than the Blendtec Classic series, but to my ears, I can’t tell. Loudness isn’t a reason to dislike a blender, however, since there is no such thing as a “quiet” blender. I wouldn’t call it overly loud.

    Blendtec blenders do not come with a tamper tool, which is essential for the Vitamix. While I do believe that a tamper tool would be helpful when using the smaller Blendtec FourSide pitcher, the larger Blendtec WildSide pitcher absolutely does not need a tamper. Watch in amazement as you pack that thing full of fruits, veggies and leafy greens and the blender just pulls it right into the spinning blades with ease.

    If you mainly make green smoothies, you’ll absolutely LOVE using a Blendtec Designer Series blender. However, it can be a challenge making nut butters and fruit-based ice creams using the WildSide or FourSide pitchers. The solution is to get the Blendtec Twister Jar, which is a fantastic accessory and takes the strain out of making nut butters, ice creams, and other thick blends.

    It also costs an additional $119.00. However, consider that a Blendtec Designer Series blender with a Twister Jar is about the same price as a Vitamix 6300, and with the Blendtec, you get more automatic pre-set blend cycles and you’ll be making nut butters and frozen-fruit ice creams much faster.

    Are You Ready To Upgrade To A Blendtec Designer Series Blender?

    If you want ultimate power and high-class style in your kitchen, then the Blendtec Designer Series blender will provide a beautiful blend of both.
